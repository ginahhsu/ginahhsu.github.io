The Problem

While people are stuck at home due to the coronavirus, travelling may seem like nothing more than a dream. Nevertheless, when things return back to a sense of normal, Airbnb wants to create new experiences for people who travel together. Our goal for the 48 hour College + Airbnb Creative Jam was to provide travellers with a place to document, organize, and share their travel experiences.

Stamplet allows friends and family to collaboratively document, organize, and share their travel experiences.

my contributions

As Stamplet was created in the span of 48 hours, my role as a UX/UI designer mainly consisted of designing and refining wireframes into prototypes. Before reaching that stage, I was in charge of researching initial pain points, target audience, and finally, my team and I brainstormed solutions.

Target Users

travellers of all ages

Ideation

Looking at the points we wanted to solve, my teammate and I decided to individually start the ideation process. Using pen and paper, we gave ourselves a two-hour limit to create low-fidelity wireframes of what we thought would solve the pain points of the challenge. Using the freshwatching technique, I wanted to use the business model of Facebook, specifically Facebook groups, and combine it with Airbnb's existing value proposition. The specific value proposition I wanted from Facebook was the ability to socialize and post articles of things they found interesting.

Product Requirements

Taking into account our target user's tasks, we picked out the key features we wanted for Stamplet:

feature one

Posting images/videos both during and after the event

To ensure that users of all ages could use our application, we wanted to have the option to instantly add photos and/or videos to your collection or to add it later on. Doing this, we can allow for instantaneous posting while also accomodating those who are less tech savvy or prefer to post together.

feature two

Gaining inspiration for trips beforehand

While some people prefer going the spontaneous route when planning for trips, we wanted users to be able to find inspiration of what to do and where to stay beforehand. Using a bookmarking system, users can quickly find their saved items in the auto-sorted country cards. As this hackathon was in collaboration with Airbnb, we wanted their experiences and accommodations to be embedded into our application for a smooth browsing process.

feature three

An archive system to store past experiences

Looking back at vacation photos is always super fun but even more so when everything is pre-sorted. Users will be able to see the locations they went to on a map, their posts throughout the trip, and even click on specific days to see what they did. These trip journals are sharable so sending them to family and friends is as easy as a click of a button.

Prototype

With all the key features and components picked out, we hopped onto the new collaboration setting for AdobeXD. The pages were split up between our team and then we were off. Within the 2 day timeline, we designed out over 30 hi-fidelity screens linked together in a working prototype.

We made sure to communicate throughout the process by calling each other with updates on our progress as well as other aspects of visual design and user experience. When it came to linking the pages, we did the sections we designed and went over it a couple of times to ensure we had the whole flow down.

Takeaways

Sprint design, brainstorming, touch areas, design system

Hackathons are always so fun to do because you're able to see the final product in such a small amount of time. However, this time constraint also taught me the difficulty of brainstorming functions that solve pain points. In the future, using HMW statements to iterate the main functions can be good for ensuring usability. On top of that, this was my first time using some sort of design system which was really exciting. One point of contention for Stamplet is the lack of touch area testing on mobile.
