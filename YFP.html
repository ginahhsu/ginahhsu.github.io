Over the span of three months, our team of four critiqued and redesigned the Young Funds Program website as the final deliverable for a course. Our objective was to help YFP create a modern and user friendly website that would help them achieve their goals of educating young adults on financial literacy.

Problem

Approximately 76% of students wish they had gotten help preparing for their personal finances. YFP tries to solve this by organizing workshops, teaching investing terms, and offering one on one classes to prepare students for the investing world.

Target

One of my team members conducted interviews with students of different investing abilities in order to further understand what they would want from a site like YFP.

Findings

We got the interviewees to run through the site and immediately there were some headaches. The original website had three main problems: unorganized content, outdated feel, and navigation problems. The information on the website was hard to retain due to these problems and finding additional information was difficult due to the navigation. Students did not want to explore the page due to the messy layout and missed out on crucial information that would guide them in their investing journey.

Phase 1: Information architecture

Our first phase was to create a sitemap in order to solve the unorganized content and navigation problems. Using this we would identify the flow of the user.

Phase 2: Medium-fidelity Wireframing

Initially, our team each made individual medium-fidelity wireframes to solve the pain points of the original website. My goal was to be able to keep the good content that the website offered but organize it in a way where the audience could easily navigate the site and find anything they wanted. I also got rid of the outdated feeling of the original site by keeping consistent colour schemes, modern fonts and designs.
Tweaks were added to our initial low-fidelity wireframes before proceeding onto high-fidelity wireframes. These tweaks included changing the order of the navigation bar to align with what the audience wanted to see as well as what YFPâ€™s goals are.

Phase 3: High-fidelity Wireframing

Our team came together with our medium-fidelity wireframes and did A/B testing in order to determine what was the best for the company to convey their information. Four rounds of testing were conducted and we created the final high-fidelity wireframe as a blueprint for our final website.

Things for the future

One of the biggest problems that we would run into if this project were to continue would be the aspect of mobile design. As we did use some bootstrap, the website was able to adjust to the different screen sizes but on mobile it would be a different story. For future sites, mobile first design would be the way to go to ensure it would be readable and functional.
